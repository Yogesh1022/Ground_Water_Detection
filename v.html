The Problem: Reactive vs. Predictive

Current government solutions (MRSAC/GSDA) provide descriptive statistics—telling us where water is missing today. They fail to warn when a specific village will run dry next season.

Vidarbha's Critical Geography

8 out of 11 districts currently face chronic groundwater depletion, creating a massive need for automated forecasting.

⚠ The Current Gap

Descriptive Limitation: Authorities react only after wells dry up.
Consequence: Emergency water tankers are deployed late, costing crores and causing crop failures mid-season.

🎯 The Proposed Solution

ML-Based Prediction: Use Multi-Modal Data Fusion with XGBoost & VAR models to forecast groundwater levels.
Outcome: 60-90 day advanced warning allowing for "Disaster Prevention" rather than "Disaster Management."

Technical Approach: The ML Pipeline

A novel Machine Learning approach combining Vector Auto-Regression (VAR) for baseline time-series trends, XGBoost (Gradient Boosted Trees) for capturing non-linear relationships, and Random Forest for ensemble validation between rainfall delays and aquifer recharge.

Monsoon Phase (Jun-Sept)
